Wedding Details

Day 1

Vratham

Before the wedding madness begins, the bride and groom slow things down with prayers and simple rituals to mentally prepare for married life. A sacred yellow thread (Kappu) is tied to their wrists. This period signifies detachment from worldly distractions and a focus on the sanctity of marriage.

Nischadartham

This is the traditional engagement where both families officially say yes and exchange blessings.
(A ceremonial “group chat announcement” that the wedding is locked and cannot be cancelled.)

Mappalai Azhaippu

The groom is ceremoniously welcomed and escorted to the wedding venue with music and family.

Day 2

Kashi Yatra

Just when things get serious, the groom dramatically decides he’s off to Kashi to become a monk and avoid marriage altogether. The bride’s father convinces him that married life is actually a better deal.
(Yes, this is scripted. No, the groom is not actually running away.)

Maalai Maathal

The bride and groom exchange garlands three times, lifted playfully by cousins and friends. It’s light-hearted yet deeply significant – it marks mutual acceptance.

Oonjal

The bride and groom are seated on a decorated swing (oonjal) and gently rocked while family members sing traditional songs and shower them with blessings.

Kanyadanam

The bride’s parents place her hand in the groom’s, symbolically entrusting her happiness to him.

Muhurtham

The main wedding moment! At the exact auspicious time, garlands are exchanged, the mangalyam is tied, and the couple officially becomes married.
(Blink and you miss it. This wedding runs on divine punctuality.)

Sapthapadi

The couple takes seven steps together, each step representing a promise they make to each other for married life.
(Seven vows. Lifetime validity. No returns or refunds.)
